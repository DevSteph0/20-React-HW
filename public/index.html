About Me

Before starting my career after high school, I enrolled at Western Washington University with hopes of trying to get into kinesiology to get a career in sports medicine which was also far away from my family and home, due to funding difficulties and lack of resources I did not have at the time I was also constantly getting parking tickets and fines for late tuition payments. Visits to the Financial Aid Office had become regular as there were ongoing issues with my financial aid. It became difficult to focus on the intense, fast-paced classes I had mistakenly chosen to take. The accumulation of stress negatively impacted my mental health and my academic progress at Western. It became increasingly difficult when the financial office made me shift my priorities from going to class to worrying about the eviction notice I kept receiving and was in turn also cut off from my meal plan. Then finally my worst fear happened, I got dismissed from Western at the start of covid due to my poor academic performance, to be frank, it hurt a lot due to I was attending my dream school and on an ultimate frisbee scholarship. After some time I moved back to Seattle where for a while I started feeling like an absolute failure which made me reflect on what I wanted to pursue in my life; Even though I was feeling down I started to apply myself to internships and met some amazing people. But at the start of fall 2022, I applied to UW Coding Bootcamp and pass the entrance exam where I was able to secure a spot where I'm currently enrolled but the same issues arose again like during my time at Western. I receive more support in other areas but I struggle to receive financial support for the school in order to pay for school I work two jobs but soon, I plan to quit one of my jobs while also working fewer hours and spending more of my time on school.
